1. Product Summary
Name: Credit Sentinel – Autonomous Credit Scoring Engine

Primary users: Credit Control Officers, Head of Credit Control

Goal: Turn CTOS PDF → extracted fields → scored decision → final approval in minutes, with full traceability and auditability.

3. Screens and Components
3.1 Screen: Dashboard
Route: /
Purpose: High-level snapshot + quick entry points.

Sections:

Header row:

Title: “Today’s Credit Pipeline”

Right: Date range selector (default: “Last 7 days”)

KPI cards (4) – single row, equal width:

“New applications today”

“Average turnaround time”

“Approval rate”

“Applications in review”

Each card: large number, small label, subtle icon.

Charts:

Left 2/3: Bar chart – count by risk category (Low/Moderate/High).

Right 1/3: Line chart – average turnaround time by day.

Table: “Applications awaiting your action”

Columns: Customer, Requested limit (RM), Risk (if scored), Status, Age, Action.

Action: [Open] button for each row.

Behaviour:

Clicking any KPI or chart segment filters the table below.

All rows clickable to open that application.

3.2 Screen: Applications Queue
Route: /applications
Purpose: Worklist for officers.

Top filters:

Status dropdown: All / New / Extracted / Scored / On Hold / Completed

Risk: All / Low / Medium / High

Search input: company name / registration no.

Date range picker (Submission date)

Main table:

Columns:

Customer / Company

Requested limit

Risk category (badge: green, amber, red, gray for “not scored yet”)

Status (New, Extracting, Ready to review, Awaiting decision, Completed)

Age (days since submission)

Last updated by

Action (primary button: “Open”)

UX notes:

Row hover highlight.

Row click also opens the application.

Status uses colored pill badges.

3.3 Screen: New Application – Upload CTOS
Route: /applications/new
Step indicator (top under title):
1) Upload CTOS → 2) Review Extraction → 3) Score & Decide

Layout:

Context card:

Left: Title “New Credit Application”

Right: small text “Step 1 of 3 – Upload CTOS report”

Main panel – two-column layout:

Left (2/3 width): File upload card

Large drag-and-drop zone:

Text: “Drop CTOS PDF here or click to browse”

Subtext: “PDF only, max 15MB”

Once file selected:

Show file name, size, pages (if available)

Show status: “Ready to upload” or “Uploading…” etc.

Right (1/3 width): Basic meta form

Fields:

Customer name (text, required)

Requested limit (numeric, RM)

Sales person (dropdown)

Notes (multiline, optional)

Actions (bottom aligned):

Secondary: “Cancel”

Primary: “Upload & Extract →” (disabled until file + customer name + requested limit filled)

State flows:

After clicking “Upload & Extract →”:

Button switches to “Processing…”

Progress message: “Extracting fields from CTOS (≈30–60 seconds)”

On success: auto-navigate to Review Extraction screen for this application.

3.4 Screen: Review Extraction
Route: /applications/:id/extraction
Purpose: Verify AI-extracted fields before scoring.

Layout: 3 columns

Left column (30%) – CTOS preview

Embedded PDF viewer:

Basic controls: page up/down, zoom, page indicator (“3/15”).

When user focuses a field in center column:

PDF viewer scrolls to relevant page.

Highlight region (approximate) in yellow overlay.

Center column (45%) – Structured fields

Group fields by section:

Company Profile

Company Name (required)

Registration No.

Incorporation Date

Address

Nature of Business

Directors & Ownership

of Directors
Directors table: Name, ID, Age, Shareholding %

Financial Strength

Paid-up Capital (required)

Net Worth

Key ratios (if extracted)

Banking Facilities (table)

Rows: Bank, Facility Type, Limit, Outstanding, Status (e.g., performing, NPL)

Risk Indicators

Litigation active? (Yes/No)

Litigation amount (if yes)

Special attention account? (Yes/No)

Bankruptcy history? (Yes/No)

For each field:

Label on left.

Value on right (readonly display with an edit icon).

Under value: small confidence chip:

“High” (green), “Medium” (orange), “Low” (red).

“View in CTOS” link to jump PDF.

Editing:

Clicking edit icon turns value into an input.

After editing, tag shows “Edited” with a pencil icon and tooltip “Edited by [user]”.

Right column (25%) – Extraction summary

“Mandatory fields” progress:

e.g., “24 / 26 mandatory fields filled”

Simple progress bar

“Low-confidence fields” list:

Shows count and clickable list (“Paid-up capital – Medium”, “Litigation – Low”) to jump to that field.

Info box:

“You must confirm all mandatory fields before scoring.”

Button:

Primary: “Confirm & Score →”

Disabled until mandatory fields are filled or explicitly marked N/A.

3.5 Screen: Score & Recommendation
Route: /applications/:id/score

Layout:

Top summary card:

Left:

Customer name, registration no.

Requested limit.

Right:

Risk category badge:

Category 1 – Low Risk (green)

Category 2 – Moderate Risk (amber)

Category 3+ – High Risk (red)

Numeric score: “Score: 77 / 100”

AI suggested decision:

“Recommendation: APPROVE up to RM 100,000”

or “Recommendation: REJECT – high litigation risk”

or “Recommendation: REVIEW – borderline profile”

Middle section – two columns

Left – Key drivers (bullet view):

Section “Strengths”:

✓ Strong paid-up capital compared to requested limit.

✓ No active litigation detected.

Section “Weaknesses / Risks”:

⚠ High gearing ratio compared to benchmark.

⚠ Limited track record with banks.

Right – Policy rules fired:

Table-like card:

Columns: Rule name | Condition | Result | Score impact

Example rows:

“Minimum Capital Rule | Paid-up ≥ RM 500k | Passed | +10”

“High Gearing Penalty | Gearing > 3.0 | Applied | -15”

Bottom controls:

Small info text:

“The score and recommendation are based on Chin Hin’s policy rules. You remain the final decision maker.”

Buttons:

Secondary: “Back to Extraction”

Primary: “Proceed to Decision →”

4. Micro-Interactions and UX Behavior
4.1 Feedback and states
Loading states:

Use skeletons for tables and cards when fetching.

Use inline spinners in buttons on long operations (upload, extract, scoring).

Empty states:

Dashboard: “No data yet – upload your first CTOS report.”

Queue: “No applications match your filters.”

Error messages:

Extraction failure: show inline error card with explanation and “Retry extraction” / “Switch to manual entry”.

Network issues: global banner at top “Connection lost, retrying…”

4.2 Accessibility
Ensure:

Sufficient color contrast (especially for risk badges).

Focus outlines for keyboard navigation.

Clear labels and aria attributes for inputs and file upload.
